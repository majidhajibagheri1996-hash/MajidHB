About

I’m a PhD candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo, working in Prof. Dayan Ban’s group at the Waterloo Institute for Nanotechnology.

My research turns everyday mechanical motion — taps, vibration, sound — into electrical signals using piezoelectric and triboelectric nanogenerators, and pairs those devices with machine learning so they can sense their surroundings without a battery.

I came to engineering from condensed-matter physics, and it still shapes how I work: model first, then build and measure. Over six years of graduate research I’ve led industry-collaborative projects with the National Research Council of Canada, Shimco North America (now Voss Innovative Technologies), and the University of Toronto, fabricated devices in the cleanroom, run first-principles simulations, and published as first author in journals including Nano Energy and Energy & Environmental Materials, with co-authorship in Nature Communications.
